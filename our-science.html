Lessons in marketing science

by Prof. Colin Skittles

Marketing Science

Marketing Science

If there’s 01 thing we nerd out on, it’s marketing science. Social is the other thing.

At Stitchy the two share a desk and probably a coffee. Let’s dig in.

A brand is a collection of memories living rent-free in people’s heads. Without attention, those memories fade and the brand withers. But with it, they compound.

Most marketing gets this wrong. It chases shiny new things. Focuses too much on what’s pretty. It shifts constantly, optimising for the moment – until ultimately nothing sticks.

Growth comes from being easy to notice and easy to remember. And that means reaching more people – not just the fans you’ve already got.

This is what marketing science gives you – a better way to understand what actually drives growth.

Most people aren’t thinking about you.

That’s the reality. Marketing isn’t persuasion – it’s being the brand that comes to mind when they’re finally ready. Stop optimising for the people already shopping. Build for the 95% who aren’t.
